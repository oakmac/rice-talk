Advice

Take this with you!

#1 Advice - Social Skills and Communication

  • Pervasive problem in our industry
  • Often has a direct impact on the software you write
  • Learn to negotiate
  • Get outside of your comfort zone
"Their [software developers] principal work is human communication to organize the user's expressions of needs into formal procedure."
Peopleware, 1987

Work at a Software Company

Work somewhere where the quality of the software effects the bottom line.

Work on "The Right Thing"

"Working on the wrong thing for a month is equivalent to not showing up to work for a month at all."
Slava Akhmechet

Working software gets you hired

  • Open source
  • Working website
  • Side projects
  • It's a smell if you don't have any of these

Tech "pop culture" considered harmful

  • Read books, not blogs
  • Focus on "timeless advice"
  • Nobody becomes an expert on distributed systems by reading Hacker News or Twitter

Education never stops

  • It's up to you
  • Knowledge is compounding
  • Power-up to your ability to create value
  • Learn by reading, but mostly doing
  • Get outside of your comfort zone

Specialize! And don't!

And finally, Congratulations!

Thank you!
